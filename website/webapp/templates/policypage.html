Building a Policy

What is a Cyber Security Policy?

A cyber security policy essentially outlines your business’s assets that require protecting, possible threats, and rules and controls to protect them and mitigate risk.

It should guide employees on how and where to share business information, use of devices and social media, and the handling, storage and deletion of sensitive materials.







1. Consider the following steps when creating a security policy for employees working from home:

  • Set Password Requirements
    • Detail the storing, updating and the importance of unique passwords
  • Outline email security measures
    • When it’s appropriate to share your work email address
    • Only opening email attachments from trusted contacts and businesses
    • Blocking junk, spam and scam emails
    • Deleting and reporting suspicious looking emails
  • Explain how to handle sensitive data
    • When employees should share sensitive data
    • How you identify what is ‘sensitive data’
    • Safe ways to delete or destroy sensitive data
  • Perform regular data backups
  • Handling technology and IT acceptable use
    • As we are working from home, where employees can access their devices such as a business laptop away from the workplace e.g. cafes
    • How to report the theft or loss of a work device
    • How system updates will be rolled out to employee devices
    • When to shut down computers and devices when not in use
    • Locking screens when computers and devices are left unattended, especially when working from home with different threats e.g. children, family members
    • How to protect the data stored on external hardware such as USB sticks
    • Restrictions on how and when to use removable devices so as to prevent malware being installed
    • Scanning all removable devices for viruses before they may be connected to the business systems
  • Social media and Internet access
    • What information is appropriate/not appropriate to share on social media platforms
    • Guidelines on what you can use your work email for e.g. signing up to new applications/accounts
    • When is it appropriate for staff to sign when using work email account
    • Guidelines around which social media platforms to access during work hours and on what devices
  • How to handle Cyber Security incidents
    • How to respond to a cyber incident
    • What are the steps to take
    • When is it appropriate for staff to sign when using work email account
    • What is your role as a staff member when dealing with a cyber attack







2. Prepare and Prevent

  • Develop a policy and procedure to help employees prevent or recognise an attack
  • Incorporate the key assets into this policy such as technology, financial assets and intellectual property
  • Consider the risks and the steps the business must take to minimise the effects of an incident
  • Create roles and responsibilities so everyone is aware of how to report an incident and the next steps







3. Check and Detect

Check and identify any unusual activities that may damage your business information and systems.
Unusual activity may include:

  • Accounts and your network not accessible
  • Passwords no longer working
  • Data is missing or altered
  • Your hard drive runs out of space
  • Your computer keeps crashing
  • Your customers receive spam from your business account







4. Identify and Access

  • Find the cause of the incident and assess the impact so you can contain it
  • Determine the impact the incident has had on your business
  • Determine its effects on your business and assets if not immediately contained







5. Respond

  • Limit further damage of the cyber incident by isolating the affected systems. If necessary, disconnect from the network and turn off your computer to stop the threat from spreading
  • Eliminate the problem with the removal of the threat
  • Recover from the incident by repairing and restoring your systems to business as usual







6. Review

  • Identify if any systems and processes need improving and make those changes
  • Evaluate the incident before and after, and any lessons learnt
  • Update your cyber security incident response plan based on the lessons learnt so you can improve your business response
