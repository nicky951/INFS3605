Detection Techniques

Key Points in Detecting Cyber Security/Social Engineering Attacks:

  • The subject of all types of phishing emails look suspicious, are vague in context and will not relate to any previous tasks completed on your device.
  • The email address of the sender looks suspicious, has the wrong domain name from the company they are claiming to be from and is long in length.
  • Phishing emails will have spelling and grammatical errors throughout as hackers prey on the uneducated and unobservant individuals.
  • Your own company will not attach unsolicited attachments and force you to download and execute files such as .exe - if any new software is to be downloaded, your IT team will do this in a physical and secure manner.
  • Malicious threat actors will ask you to download Remote Access Tools (RAT) such as TeamViewer, LogMeIn and AnyDesk.
  • Any suspicious URLs in phishing emails will display a non-legitimate website link and will not begin with “https://.”
